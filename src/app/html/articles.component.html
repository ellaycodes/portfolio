A theoretical model for measuring CSR/sustainability reports quality.

Business & Society, May 2019, SAGE Publications

DOI: 10.1177/0007650319843623

This article presents a semiotics-based model to evaluate the quality of CSR reports by analyzing the language used. The model helps assess the transparency and accuracy of CSR communication, offering both qualitative and quantitative tools to improve corporate sustainability reporting.

Making sense of CSR reports the semiotic way

Business & Society, November 2023, Journal Article

This page discusses how semiotics can enhance corporate social responsibility (CSR) communication by analyzing language and symbolism to convey authentic values, building stakeholder trust and improving investment or customer loyalty.

Making Sense of CSR Reports: A Methodological Perspective from Semiotics

Encyclopedia of Sustainable Management. Springer

https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-02006-4_1159-1

This page highlights the use of semiotics to assess the quality of corporate social responsibility (CSR) reports, focusing on how language and symbols reveal corporate transparency and ethical values. It helps companies align their messaging with societal expectations to build trust.
